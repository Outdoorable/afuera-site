Using Repeatable AI Workflows for Iconic Trip Ops

What I set up for the recent trip in CDMX to make life easier, and a few thoughts on what it could look like at Iconic at scale to save time, cut costs, and improve customer experience.

Ali Murphy · Afuera · April 2026
What I did

A personal hub in Claude co-work to manage everything involving research, FAM, during and post trip. With much of it automated.

I lead trips for Iconic and live in Mexico City, so for Atlas 400 I started using Claude Cowork on my laptop — connected to my Drive and Gmail — as a kind of personal search engine for the trip. Dropped in every doc Maureen and Amber sent me, every email thread, every vendor contract, and let it index all of it.

Once that was set up, I could ask plain questions like "has Salon de Agave confirmed the new time?" or "what did we promise Jacaranda?" and get an answer pulled from the actual source.

From there it became a Hub for managing every aspect of the trip — a system of custom tools and skills, each one built as I needed it. Some quick — a skill that reads tomorrow's itinerary and drafts the daily WhatsApp to guests. Some more elaborate — a full expense tracker I'd love every leader to have.

Seven skills I built to run the trip

that can be reusable plugins for any teammate

Skill 01

FAM Companion

See demo
What it did for me

Aggregated the master trip doc, the dietary and accessibility notes, the client profiles, and every vendor email thread into one shared FAM picture. Built the schedule across what Amber set up, what Lizelle set up, and what I added. Generated per-vendor briefs the night before each visit — talking points, dietary flags relevant to that stop, the exact questions I needed answered before walking in. Replaced the "wait, who's visiting Salon de Agave on Tuesday?" chaos with one shared sheet.

What it could do

Live coordination during FAM days — who's running late, who needs backup, whose visit is over and what they learned. Automated brief regeneration when something changes (new dietary note from a guest at 9pm → updated brief flagged for the breakfast vendor at 6am). Debrief auto-draft from voice notes captured at each stop, so the post-FAM writeup writes itself.

Skill 02

Expense Tracker

See demo
What it did for me

This one got more elaborate than I expected. Pre-trip, it walked me through setup — uploaded Iconic's expense training PDF and the trip-specific spending guidelines, configured my cards, the cash advance amount, my co-leader. During the trip, a photo of any receipt plus a 5-second voice note ("Contramar, FAM lunch for four, my Amex, 200 peso cash tip") got parsed, currency-converted, mapped to the right Expensify category, and filed in the right report. Cash tips that would have been forgotten got logged. Post-trip, it ran a full audit — flagged FX gaps between POS amount and card settlement (I had a $1.75 gap on the Apr 8 Ritz bill that took me an hour to figure out), found tips I hadn't logged against the spending budget, generated my "Questions for Mo" doc, and exported Expensify-ready reports.

What it could do

Shared live budget across leaders — Lizelle and I see in real time who paid what and what's left. Automatic dedup on shared tips so the Casa Jacaranda $300 doesn't get claimed twice across leaders. Per-trip margin visibility for finance the moment expenses post.

Skill 03

Daily Snapshot

What it did for me

Each morning during Atlas 400, it read the day's itinerary from the trip workbook, pulled CDMX weather, cross-referenced traffic on the routes I'd be using, flagged anything worth shifting (rain moving up, traffic worse than usual on Insurgentes), and drafted the daily WhatsApp message to guests in my voice. I'd review it for 30 seconds and send.

What it could do

Generate per-guest personalized notes when the group has subgroups — a wine collector and a Spanish-only speaker get different briefs. Push proactive contingency suggestions when weather or traffic crosses a threshold ("rain moved up to 3pm — the Jacaranda courtyard plan needs to flip to indoor, here's the script").

Skill 04

Arrivals & Departures

What it did for me

Watched pax flight numbers from the manifest. When a flight changed — delay, early arrival, gate change — Claude flagged it, drafted an update for Julia at the Ritz to shift check-in timing, prepped a heads-up for the driver, and auto-generated a fresh arrivals PDF with the changes baked in — instantly. It also caught the terminal-grouping issue — that our Apr 9 arrivals were split across three terminals, which meant an extra car we hadn't budgeted.

What it could do

Auto-rebook ground transport when delays cross set thresholds. Push proactive WhatsApp updates to guests ("we've adjusted your pickup — no action needed on your end").

Skill 05

Know Your Area (KOA)

What it did for me

Each morning, Claude read tomorrow's stops and handed me 3 interesting talking points per venue — pulled from web research, cross-referenced with the trip context. When I walked guests into Mesa Meroma I had three real things to weave in beyond "this place is great."

What it could do

Personalize per-guest — a pax with a wine collection gets a different KOA at Salon de Agave than one who's into Mexican textile history. Cross-reference Iconic's institutional memory — Pedro at Meroma's signature pairing from a past trip's debrief gets surfaced before the visit.

Skill 06

Client Info On Demand

What it did for me

Mid-trip, mid-conversation, I could ask "remind me of Kate's dietary restrictions" or "what's [pax]'s history with Iconic" and get the full dossier in 5 seconds. Pulled from the master profiles plus past trip notes if they were a returning client.

What it could do

Surface preferences proactively during vendor coordination ("Kate prefers earlier dinner times — schedule Meroma at 7 not 8"). The institutional memory layer — every leader knowing what any leader noticed across trips.

Skill 07

Vendor Email Sweep

What it did for me

Each night, Claude scanned my inbox for vendor threads, classified them (confirmed, pending reply, gone silent, action-needed), and dropped a morning summary on me — Pedro at Meroma confirmed corkage waiver. Salon de Agave still hasn't responded. Maria from Liderazgo wants to confirm pickup time. I started each day knowing exactly who needed a nudge.

What it could do

Auto-draft the nudge emails for stale threads in my voice. Parse vendor commitments into the master tracker the moment confirmation lands — no manual data entry.

How this could look for Iconic

One route you might go to scale efficiency

Take Iconic from individual optimization and skill sharing to major efficiency gains, better transparency for the office, less coordination tax — and real dollars, too.

  1. Iconic gets one Claude Enterprise workspace. Single bill, your admin (probably Amber or ops). Everyone gets a seat — Maureen, Amber, leaders as they're assigned to trips.
  2. The skills above bundle into one Iconic Trip Operator plugin. Your admin pushes it — every leader has them on day one.
  3. It connects into your existing stack, not around it. Files stay in Drive (Trip Workbooks, manifests, vendor docs — all of it). Gmail stays in Gmail. Calendar stays in Calendar. The plugin reads from and writes back to those — nothing new to migrate.
  4. Each trip becomes its own shared Project — Amber, the assigned leaders, finance (read-only). Files in Drive, the Project is the live working layer over them.
  5. If you wanted to try it, the cleanest first move is one trip end-to-end on the system. Pick the next one, run it on this, see what holds up.
Two things worth flagging

Two things I'd think about

01

Adaptability is brutally low on individual accounts.

One person can build great skills, but if everyone has their own personal Claude account, sharing happens manually, updates don't propagate, and adoption stalls in single digits. The team that builds the skills isn't the team that needs them most.

With Enterprise, the admin pushes skills to everyone — adoption is opt-out, not opt-in. That's the only thing that gets actual usage past the first month.

02

Security on individual accounts means you have no control.

If leaders use personal Claude accounts, Iconic's docs sit on personal laptops in personal tenancies. No audit log Iconic can see, no kill switch when someone stops working with you, no way to revoke access.

With Enterprise, Iconic owns the workspace. Audit logs are yours. Seat revocation is instant. Data sits in Iconic's tenancy, not on anyone's personal device. SOC 2, ISO 27001, BAA, zero data retention all available.

The skills only matter if everyone actually uses them, and your data only matters if you control it. Both need Enterprise — neither is a "we'll figure it out later" thing.

On other AI tools

If you're wondering about Gemini, ChatGPT, or building custom

Gemini · Google

Best Drive and Gmail integration of any of them — that's where it shines. Falls short on what I built because it doesn't have a Cowork-equivalent (desktop file editing plus skills as installable, repeatable tools every leader uses). You can do trip Q&A on your Drive in Gemini. You can't easily ship Snapshot, the Expense Tracker, or the FAM Companion as installable tools. (Microsoft Copilot is the equivalent for the M365 stack — same logic if you ever moved there.)

ChatGPT Enterprise

GPTs are similar to Claude skills. Mature ecosystem, lots of integrations. The catch: GPTs are web-only — they can't edit local files the way Cowork does. The "snap a receipt, voice-note a tip, file goes into the tracker" workflow doesn't translate cleanly. Possible to rebuild, but it's a different stack.

Custom software

Probably the right answer eventually — not yet. Custom build costs are down ~80% since 2022. At Afuera we run builds at that rate — one senior engineer controlling a team of agents that moves 10x faster, with the same diligence. A bespoke Iconic platform — mobile-native, client-facing, owned outright — becomes more viable every quarter. Worth revisiting after 6–12 months on the Claude version when you actually know what's working and what's not.
