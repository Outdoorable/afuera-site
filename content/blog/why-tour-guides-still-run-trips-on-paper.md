A guide I'll call Marcus folded a printed spreadsheet into the back pocket of his hiking pants on a Monday morning in Oaxaca. Twenty guests. Seven dietary restrictions. Three hotel confirmations the office had sent to the wrong email address and then fixed at 11 PM the night before. Two co-leaders, one van driver, one WhatsApp group that had gone from useful to chaotic around guest seven.

By lunch on Day 1, the spreadsheet had a coffee ring on it and a handwritten note in the margin about Carla, who is allergic to salmon and had already been served salmon.

This is not a story about Marcus being bad at his job. Marcus is one of the best guides in the country.
