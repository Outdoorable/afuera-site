Hi — Ali here. Welcome to the Afuera blog.

This is where I'll share what I'm learning, building, and breaking as I help travel businesses fold AI into their day-to-day. Short posts, honest posts. No thought-leadership puff.

## Who this is for

If you're running a tour operation, a small tour company, a solo travel advisory, or a bespoke trip design studio — and your Tuesday looks like twenty-seven browser tabs, four Excel files, and a guide calling you from the side of a road in Costa Rica because the supplier didn't send the van — this blog is for you.

I spent four years leading active travel trips. I've built bespoke itinerary services. I've rebuilt a global tour operator's entire marketing operation from scratch. I've worked in travel ops and I've built AI systems across tech, logistics, SaaS, and e-commerce. The patterns that work in those industries are mostly the same patterns that work in travel — we're just a few years behind, and that's actually good news.

## What you can expect here

- **Specific workflows** — not "you should use AI!" but "here's how I replaced a 4-hour Monday task with a 7-minute one."
- **Candid tradeoffs** — when AI helps, when it doesn't, and when you should just hire a person instead.
- **Travel-native examples** — built around the actual operations of tour companies, advisors, and designers. Not generic SaaS case studies dressed up in wanderlust copy.

## What you won't get

- Hype. "AI will revolutionize everything" posts are cheap. I'd rather tell you what worked on a real trip last Tuesday.
- Endless tool reviews. I'll mention tools when they're relevant but the focus is systems, not software shopping.
- Gated content. Everything here is free, open, and copyable. If a post gives you an idea, steal it.

## How to use this blog

A few suggestions, roughly in order:

1. Skim the TL;DR at the top of each post (it's the summary from the frontmatter). If it's relevant to your week, read on.
2. Use the jump links if the post is long — each H2 becomes a TOC entry.
3. FAQs at the bottom answer the most common questions I get when friends read a post.
4. If you want to talk through a specific problem, grab a slot on my calendar. The button at the top of the page goes to a free 45-minute call. No pitch.

## What's next

First real post will be about the most common misunderstanding I hear from travel operators when they say they "want to use AI" — and why starting with an audit instead of a pilot changes everything.

Thanks for reading the first one. See you in a couple days.

— Ali
